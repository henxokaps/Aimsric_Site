ACADEMICS

Welcome to the Academic Department of AIMS RIC! We are dedicated to fostering educational self-sufficiency in the mathematical sciences across Africa. Our aim is to develop a robust pipeline of highly trained professionals who can contribute significantly to the continent's scientific and technological advancement.

We envision an Africa where expertise in mathematical sciences is both deep and widespread. To achieve this, we focus on producing graduates at both the Master's and PhD levels, with an emphasis on the latter. PhD-level experts play a crucial role in our strategy as they serve as teachers and mentors, creating a strong multiplying effect that accelerates the growth of knowledge and expertise across the continent.

The Academic Department is led by the Academic Manager, supported by the Research and Academic Coordinator. Activities in the Academic Department are carried out in consultation with, and with guidance from the AIMS Global Network Team of Academic Directors, The AIMS Global Network Chief Academic Officer, and the AIMS International Scientific Advisor. This dedicated team of professionals is committed to advancing mathematical sciences education and research in Africa. They ensure that the department operates smoothly, fosters innovation, and provides the highest quality of support for the education of the students.

Our academic programs are designed to provide rigorous training and research opportunities that prepare students for leadership roles in academia, industry, and government. These programs still focus on the centre’s core areas of research, that is, Data, Quantum, and Modeling. We offer:

Research Masters Program

With support from different funders, AIMS RIC occasionally opens calls for applications from students who are either in their final year of a BSc program or hold a BSc degree with a keen interest in Data Science, Quantum Science, and Mathematical Modeling. This incorporates related disciplines such as Mathematics, Statistics, Physics, Machine Learning, Cluster Analysis, Data Mining, Big Data Analytics, Data Visualization, Artificial Intelligence, Neural Networks, Deep Learning, Deep Active Learning, and Cognitive Computing among others. Applicants holding an MSc degree may also qualify for a one-year MPhil program. The fellowship duration is normally up to 24 months with additional funding to attend conferences and international scientific events.

To qualify for a Research Masters position at AIMS RIC, one has to fit the following eligibility criteria;

  • Applicants from any African country are welcome to apply.
  • Applicants must possess either a minimum of a BSc degree in a field related to mathematical sciences by the position's start date. Also, an MSc degree holder in a field related to mathematical sciences may qualify for a one-year MPhil program.
  • Interest in conducting research in one of our core areas of research and/or related disciplines is essential.
  • Applicants should demonstrate openness and willingness to research topics proposed by their respective supervisors.

As part of the application process, candidates are asked to prepare responses to the following questions:

  • Why do you think you should be selected for this fellowship?
  • How has your background prepared you for this fellowship?
  • How is this fellowship aligned with your career goals and future aspirations?
  • Which African Scientist do you mulate or who inspires you? State why and how?
Doctoral Training Program

In 2019, AIMS started a Doctoral Training Centre (DTC) to develop PhD-level experts. The need for such a centre was made evident by the institution’s internal assessment of the uncoordinated manner in which PhD students had been trained. The centre was established to strengthen research in Africa, with the vision to catalyze human progress through the creation, translation, and dissemination of knowledge;

  • Creating new knowledge, including solutions to relevant problems.
  • Converting and interpreting data into useful forms in addressing society’s challenges.
  • Effective dissemination of knowledge via, for example, journal publications and other media.

The first Doctoral Training Program (DTP) was in Climate Science, and it has already elevated to a much higher level than before the quality of the learning opportunities available to AIMS students. In 2021, AIMS started the second DTP, in Data Science, followed by one in Climate and Health Interactions, and in Malaria Modelling, both in 2023. The next step is to create another DTP in Quantum Science.

AIMS RIC has a very small faculty, so it cannot train as many PhD students as it wants, to the required quality, by relying only on its staff. For future DTPs, we are looking at recruiting people to provide the necessary academic support. In addition, AIMS will continue to rely on a network of external faculty for student (co)supervision. Furthermore, for sustainability, AIMS needs to diversify the sources of funding available to the DTPs. For all these reasons, AIMS RIC needs strong institutional partners to provide support across the board - from planning to fundraising to execution. A lot of work has already gone into developing the required structures for the aforementioned DTPs, and this provides an excellent platform to continue to build on.

Standard requirements and qualifications for the DTP
Following the AIMS RIC PhD student recruitment policy, the program calls for applicants from candidates with the following qualifications;

  • Master’s degree (completed by Sept) in Mathematics, Statistics, Computer Science, Quantum, Climate Science, Modeling, and/or other relevant fields;
  • Sufficient theoretical foundations evidenced by prior work (courses, theses, or other training);
  • Qualification for pursuing research on the chosen topic, including relevant programming expertise;
  • Research potential evidenced by academic performance and involvement in relevant academic activities;
  • Motivation to pursue a PhD by research in a topic related to AIMS RIC’s focus areas of research;
  • Being an African national.
Application process

AIMS RIC follows a standard procedure when recruiting suitable candidates for DTPs. The student recruitment process follows the procedure below;

  • A call for topic submissions from potential supervisors with innovative and impactful research topics that address challenges in the fields of interest.
  • After thorough evaluation, selected topics are uploaded on the website, ensuring that they are accessible to all interested parties.
  • A call for applications for interested students follows. The students select a maximum of 2 topics from the proposed topics but also have a chance to propose their own topics.
  • The received applications are verified for relevant application documents and sent for evaluation and ranking. This rigorous assessment ensures that only the most qualified and motivated candidates are selected to participate in the program. Unsuccessful candidates are sent either generic or personalised emails.
  • Shortlisted candidates’ application packages are then forwarded to proposed supervisors for evaluation.
  • Successful applicants are asked to collaborate with their supervisors to develop full proposals, which are subsequently reviewed before submission to the selection committee.
  • Successful proposals receive offer letters, followed by an onboarding process and the official commencement of their positions as AIMS RIC Ph.D. students.

PhD students are mentored by a team of 2-4 supervisors, creating a partnership between AIMS and higher education institutions both in Africa and internationally. Each supervision team must include at least one supervisor affiliated with AIMS and one affiliated with the graduating institution based in Africa. These guidelines are flexible and can be adjusted on a case-by-case basis.

Student life

The DTP student evaluation process at AIMS RIC is designed to ensure rigorous academic progress and high standards of scientific research. Each PhD student is granted a 36-month fellowship signed in acceptance together with a structured supervisor agreement. The evaluation process includes the submission of quarterly and annual reports, along with mid-year and end-year evaluations during which their progress is closely monitored. This systematic approach aims to maintain scientific rigor and quality control, ensuring that all students meet the program's objectives and contribute meaningfully to their respective fields.

Following the requirements of the graduating institution, each student is supported to take extra courses covering fundamental and advanced topics in relevant fields, for example, data science, quantum science, and modelling. We also provide support for students to take specialised courses that allow them to tailor their education to their research interests.

Training programs: The annual Doctoral Training School (DTS) in foundational methods in data science is a four-week highly interactive session intended for early-stage African PhD students working in data science, broadly defined to include applied mathematics, mathematical modelling, statistics, computer science, machine learning, and Artificial Intelligence. This DTS aims to address the skills gap that many African students face when transitioning from taught postgraduate education into PhD research.

The DTS focuses on;

  • Core Courses such as Machine Learning Essentials, Problem Solving with Data Science, Statistics and Scientific Methods, and Optimization for Data Science Applications
  • Special Topics Courses like Introduction to R, Computational Methods for Random Effect Models, Introduction to Generative Modeling, Large Language Models, Functional Data Analysis: Exploring Frontiers and Beyond, and Fundamentals of Reinforcement Learning
  • Transferable skills training, for example, Presentation Skills, Research Methodology, Gender Equality Diversity & Inclusion, and Science for Social Good

Lecturers for the DTS are normally selected from a pool of AIMS Alumni, industry experts (Air Liquid, IBM, etc), and global Academic experts. The DTS targets participants from the new cohort of the AIMS RIC DTP, 5 – 10 scholarship students from African countries, and 5 – 10 local PhD students from Rwanda.

The Theory to Practise (T2P) Workshop in Data Science aims to provide a platform to discuss and explore data science topics that are relevant to solving problems on the African continent, in particular.
